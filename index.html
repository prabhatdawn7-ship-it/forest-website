Forest Cover Change in Palamau Tiger Reserve

Palamau Tiger Reserve is located in Jharkhand, India.
It was established in 1973 under Project Tiger.
Forest cover has changed over time due to deforestation, climate change and human activities.

About the Reserve

Palamau Tiger Reserve is rich in biodiversity and tropical dry deciduous forest.
Remote Sensing and GIS techniques are used to monitor forest cover change.
Satellite imagery helps in analyzing land use and deforestation patterns.
